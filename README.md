# HR Analytics – Employee Attrition Analysis & Prediction

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations as it results in increased hiring costs, reduced productivity, loss of experienced talent, and operational disruptions. Understanding why employees leave and identifying individuals at risk of attrition can help organizations improve retention strategies and workforce planning.

This project focuses on analyzing employee data to identify the key factors influencing attrition and building predictive models to estimate the likelihood of employees leaving the organization. Using Python for data analysis and machine learning, along with Tableau for visualization, the project provides actionable insights to support data-driven HR decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Organizations often struggle to understand the reasons behind employee turnover. High attrition rates can negatively impact business performance, increase recruitment expenses, and reduce team productivity.

The objective of this project is to analyze employee-related data, identify the major drivers of attrition, and develop predictive models that help HR departments proactively address employee retention challenges.

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Employees working overtime showed significantly higher attrition rates.
- Lower job satisfaction levels were strongly associated with employee turnover.
- Certain job roles experienced higher attrition compared to others.
- Employees with lower income levels were more likely to leave the organization.
- Work-life balance played an important role in employee retention.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Improve employee work-life balance initiatives.
- Monitor employees with excessive overtime.
- Develop retention strategies for high-risk departments and job roles.
- Enhance employee engagement and satisfaction programs.
- Implement data-driven workforce planning and retention policies.
